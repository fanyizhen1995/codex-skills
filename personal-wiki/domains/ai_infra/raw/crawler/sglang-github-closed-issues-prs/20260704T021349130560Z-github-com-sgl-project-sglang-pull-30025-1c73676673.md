# fix: reorder DSA indexer dual-stream ops to avoid CUDA graph stream explosion

URL: https://github.com/sgl-project/sglang/pull/30025
State: closed
Labels: run-ci
Closed at: 2026-07-03T20:53:17Z
Merged at: 2026-07-03T20:53:17Z

## Motivation

On GLM-5.2 (DSA) with EAGLE speculative decoding, the profiler shows ~22 CUDA
streams during target verify instead of the expected 2. This appeared after the
DSA indexer Q/K fusion landed.

The root cause is the issue order of the dual-stream section in the fused
indexer path `Indexer._fused_q_prepare_and_store`. The second overlap stage
enqueued the alt-stream fused-K + KV-cache store **before** the main-stream
fused-Q kernel. During CUDA-graph capture, that ordering makes
`cudaGraphInstantiate` reserve a fresh side stream for each layer's fork
(~22 total) instead of reusing a single one.

## Modification

Reorder the second overlap stage in `_fused_q_prepare_and_store` so the
main-stream fused-Q kernel is enqueued **before** the alt-stream fused-K +
KV-cache store. This is a pure scheduling reorder — no numerics change — and it
lets graph capture reuse one alt stream, collapsing the captured graph from
~22 streams back to 2 while preserving the Q‖K dual-stream overlap
(max concurrent worker = 2).

## Benchmark

GLM-5.2-NVFP4, TP4, EAGLE (steps 5 / topk 1 / draft 6), `generated-shared-prefix`
(sys 73360 / q 6640), output-len 220, `--max-concurrency 1`. Each config was
launched once and benchmarked **N=10** times; Welch's t-test vs HEAD.

Note: `TPOT = per_step_cost / accept_len`, and `accept_len` has a genuine
run-to-run noise floor (~±3%) from non-deterministic GPU reductions in spec
decoding, so the confound-free comparison is per-step verify cost
(`TPOT x accept_len`).

**Accept length** (N=10)

| config | mean | std | vs HEAD | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| HEAD (fused, 22 streams) | 2.810 | 0.079 | — | — |
| fusion-off (`SGLANG_DISABLE_DSA_INDEXER_FUSION=1`) | 2.803 | 0.094 | -0.26% (t=+0.19) | noise |
| **fix (reorder, 2 streams)** | 2.865 | 0.087 | +1.96% (t=-1.48) | noise |

**Mean TPOT (ms)** (N=10)

| config | mean | std | vs HEAD | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| HEAD | 4.208 | 0.127 | — | — |
| fusion-off | 4.345 | 0.154 | +3.26% (t=-2.17) | slower |
| **fix** | 4.133 | 0.115 | -1.78% (t=+1.39) | noise |

**Per-step verify cost = TPOT x accept_len (ms)** — confound-free, high power

| config | mean | std | vs HEAD | t | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| HEAD | 11.817 | 0.068 | — | — | — |
| fusion-off | 12.167 | 0.134 | +2.96% | -7.37 | genuinely slower |
| **fix** | 11.833 | 0.087 | **+0.14%** | -0.47 | **identical to HEAD** |

Conclusion: the reorder is **performance-neutral vs HEAD** (per-step +0.14%,
not significant) while collapsing the captured graph from 22 -> 2 streams.
Keeping fusion is still worthwhile (fusion-off is ~3% slower, t=-7.37).
